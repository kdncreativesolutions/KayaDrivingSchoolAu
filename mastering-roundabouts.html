Mastering Roundabouts: A Canberra Specialty

Canberra is famous for its roundabouts. For many new drivers, they can be the most intimidating part of the road.

If you're learning to drive in Canberra, you'll encounter roundabouts of all shapes and sizes—from tiny suburban ones to massive multi-lane intersections. Understanding the rules and building confidence is essential for passing your test and driving safely in the ACT.

The Golden Rule: Give Way to the Right

The most important rule is to give way to any vehicle already in the roundabout. This includes vehicles coming from your right. However, don't just look right—keep an eye on all entrances to anticipate traffic flow.

Signaling correctly

Always signal your intention as you approach. If turning left, signal left. If turning right, signal right. If going straight, you don't need a signal on approach, but you **MUST** signal left as you exit (where practical) to let others know you're leaving.

Lane Discipline

In multi-lane roundabouts, your lane choice is critical. Generally, use the left lane for turning left or going straight, and the right lane for turning right or going straight (check the arrows on the road). Once you've chosen a lane, stay in it throughout the roundabout. Changing lanes inside a roundabout is highly discouraged and can be dangerous.

Bicycles and Pedestrians

Watch out for cyclists who may be using the roundabout. Give them plenty of space. Also, be aware that some roundabouts have pedestrian crossings immediately at the entrance or exit. Always be prepared to stop if someone is crossing.

Practical Tips for Success

The key to mastering roundabouts is **smoothness**. Approach at a speed that allows you to stop if necessary, but keep moving if the gap is safe. Indecision can cause confusion for other drivers.
