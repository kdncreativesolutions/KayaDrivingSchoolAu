How to Get a Driving Licence in Canberra, ACT

Your complete step-by-step guide to navigating the ACT licensing system.

Introduction

Getting a driver licence in the ACT is an exciting and important step toward becoming a safe and confident driver. Whether you are a beginner applying for your learner plates, an overseas licence holder, or transferring from another Australian state, understanding the ACT licensing process can save you time and help you avoid confusion.

The official licensing authority in Canberra is Access Canberra, and they oversee all aspects of driver testing and licence issuing.

1. Learner Licence (L Plates)

To apply for a learner licence in Canberra, ACT, you must:

  • Be at least 15 years and 9 months old.
  • Complete the ACT Pre-Learner Licence Course.
  • Pass the Road Rules Knowledge Test.
  • Provide proof of identity and ACT residency.
  • Visit an Access Canberra Service Centre.

Once approved, you will receive your ACT Learner Licence (L Plates).

Official Guide: Get Your Learner Licence

2. Learner Licence Requirements

While driving with a learner licence, you must always drive with a fully licensed supervising driver and clearly display L plates on your vehicle. You must also follow all ACT road rules and complete the required supervised driving hours before progressing.

Required Driving Hours

Age Group Required Driving Hours
Under 25 years 100 supervised hours (including 10 night hours)
25 years and older 50 supervised hours (including 5 night hours)

3. Hazard Perception Test (HPT)

Before moving to a provisional licence, learner drivers must complete the Hazard Perception Test (HPT). The HPT helps drivers recognise dangerous traffic situations and make safer driving decisions. Drivers can usually take the HPT after holding their learner licence for at least 3 months.

Hazard Perception Test Information

4. Provisional Licence (P Plates)

To progress from L Plates to P Plates in the ACT, you must:

  • Hold your learner licence for 12 months (if under 25 years old) or 6 months (if 25 years or older).
  • Complete the required logbook hours.
  • Pass the Hazard Perception Test.
  • Successfully complete Competency Based Training & Assessment (CBT&A) with an accredited instructor, OR pass a one-off practical driving test.
Official Guide: Get Your Provisional Licence

5. Full Driver Licence in ACT

Drivers can upgrade to a full ACT licence after completing their provisional licence period. Generally, drivers must hold a provisional licence for at least 3 years, maintain a good driving record, and meet any applicable medical or legal requirements.

6. Overseas Licence Holders in Canberra

If you move to Canberra from another country, the process depends on whether your country is recognised by Australia.

Recognised Countries

Drivers from recognised countries may be able to convert their overseas licence directly to a full ACT licence without additional driving tests. You will typically need your overseas licence, passport or photo identification, proof of ACT residency, and an English translation of your licence (if required).

Non-Recognised Countries

Drivers from non-recognised countries may need to pass the ACT Road Rules Knowledge Test and a practical driving assessment. If unsuccessful, drivers may need to enter the ACT learner licence system.

Drivers With Overseas Licences

7. Interstate Licence Transfers

If you move to Canberra from another Australian state or New Zealand, you must transfer your licence to an ACT licence within the required timeframe (usually within 3 months). You will generally need your current driver licence, proof of ACT residency, identification documents, and to pay the licence transfer fee.

Drivers With Interstate Licences

8. Important Documents Required

Most ACT licence applications require:

  • Passport or photo ID.
  • Proof of ACT residential address.
  • Existing driver licence (if applicable).
  • Medical documents (if requested).
  • English translation for overseas licences.

Tip: Always check the latest requirements on the Access Canberra website before visiting a Service Centre.

9. Tips for Passing Your Driving Test

  • Practice regularly in different traffic conditions.
  • Study Canberra road rules carefully.
  • Always check mirrors and blind spots.
  • Maintain a safe speed and correct lane position.
  • Practice parking, roundabouts, and intersections.
  • Stay calm and confident during the driving test.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

How much does an ACT learner licence cost?

Fees change slightly each year, but you'll generally pay for the road rules knowledge test, the pre-learner course, and the physical licence card. Always check Access Canberra for the current fee schedule.

Can I drive in other states with my ACT learner licence?

Yes, you can drive in other Australian states on your ACT learner licence, provided you comply with both the ACT learner rules and the local rules of the state you are driving in.

How long is the Hazard Perception Test valid for?

Once you pass the HPT in the ACT, it is generally valid for the duration of your learner licence or until you successfully obtain your provisional licence.
