Navigating Night Driving in Canberra

Driving at night presents unique challenges, especially with Canberra's layout and local wildlife.

In the ACT, gaining experience driving at night is a mandatory part of your learner logbook (20 hours for learners under 25). But beyond the requirements, nighttime driving requires a different set of skills and heightened awareness. Here's how to stay safe on Canberra's roads after dark.

The "Kangaroo Factor"

Canberra is the bush capital, and our roads often intersect with nature reserves. Kangaroos are most active at dawn and dusk, but they are a significant hazard throughout the night. If you're driving on arterial roads like the Tuggeranong Parkway or Majura Parkway, keep a sharp eye on the grassy verges.

Pro Tip: High Beam Etiquette

Use your high beams on unlit roads to increase your visibility, but remember to switch back to low beams as soon as you see an oncoming vehicle or are following someone. Blinding other drivers is dangerous and a common cause of accidents.

Dealing with Glare

Glare from oncoming headlights can temporarily blind you. To minimize the effect, avoid looking directly at the lights. Instead, focus your eyes slightly towards the left side of your lane (the "fog line" or kerb). This helps you maintain your position while your peripheral vision handles the oncoming traffic.

Visibility and Speed

Your "stopping distance" should always be within the range of your headlights. At night, your perception of depth and speed is reduced. Slow down slightly, increase your following distance to at least 4 seconds, and ensure your windshield is clean—streaks on the glass can cause massive glare issues at night.

Final Advice

If you're a learner driver, don't do all your night hours in one go. Start with short trips on familiar, well-lit suburban streets before progressing to faster, darker roads.
